Today I'm at my church's encounter, which is where they invite new believers to "encounter" God. Not really sure why so come here every Sunday but there is something about seeing familiar people once a week that is comforting. It's also an environment out of home, so maybe it feels that way because of this.

![speaker at church](/assets/image.jpg)


I'm on the praise team, and I play Bass, but as far as I seem to know I am literally just in a band playing Bass. The words don't really register, and I don't like to think about them. I'm really just playing, and playing to play because I enjoy music.

The message was interesting to listen to. I think Christianity makes sense, God can be just and loving. Here's the story he provided.

> A King is a just king, and he loves his mother. His mother commits a crime, and he must punish her with 40 lashes by a whip. The king orders her to be tied up to a post, and he walks down, takes his robe off, and receives the punishment for her, while embracing her.

This is a common story told, to show that God really can be just and loving. Interesting to think about.

In terms of religion and **me**, I just don't like to think about it. Maybe it's because I'm negligent of the truth, or maybe I'd rather not live in constant thinking. I think I'm afraid of one true truth honestly.
